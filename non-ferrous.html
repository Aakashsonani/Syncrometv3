Non-Ferrous Metals

The high-value metals that move markets

Non-ferrous metals contain no iron, resist corrosion and are non-magnetic — and they command premium prices. We sort, assay and grade every load to get you the most for it.

What We Accept

Nine non-ferrous streams, expertly graded

Because non-ferrous values are high and grade-sensitive, our specialists sort and assay every load — so your payout reflects exactly what you brought in.

Copper

Most valuable common metal. Accepted: bare bright, #1 copper, #2 copper, insulated wire, tubing, sheet, bus bar, roofing copper, HVAC components.

Aluminum

100% recyclable; 92% energy saving vs primary. Accepted: extrusions, castings, cans, siding, wheels, wire, sheet, radiators.

Brass

Alloy of copper and zinc. Accepted: yellow brass, red brass, mixed brass, shell casings, fittings, valves.

Stainless Steel

Accepted: 304, 316, 430 grades — sinks, pipes, industrial equipment, cutlery. Tested for nickel content.

Lead

Accepted: wheel weights, roofing lead, pipes, battery plates (lead-acid). Handled under strict environmental controls.

Zinc

Accepted: die-cast zinc, galvanized scrap, zincs from industrial processes.

Nickel

Accepted: nickel-bearing alloys, superalloys from aerospace and industrial use.

Catalytic Converters

Contain platinum, palladium, rhodium — high-value recovery. Assayed individually with full documentation.

Specialty Alloys

Titanium, Inconel, Monel — valued by aerospace and defense industries. Call us to discuss.
